Venus de Milo

Artist: Alexandros of Antioch

4.7/5

The Venus de Milo is an ancient Greek statue, believed to represent Aphrodite, the goddess of love and beauty. Discovered in 1820 on the island of Milos, it is admired for its classical Greek elegance, despite its missing arms.


Year:c. 150 BCE

Medium: Marble

Dimensions: 204 cm (6 ft 8 in)

Location: Louvre Museum, Paris

Price: $467 Billion
